The oldest find of a money box dates from 200 BC Greek colony Priene, Asia Minor, and features the shape of a little Greek temple with a slit in the pediment. Money boxes of various forms were also excavated in Pompeii and Herculaneum, and appear quite frequently on late ancient provincial sites, particularly in Roman Britain and along the Rhine.


By the 15th century, terracotta piggy banks were made in all shapes and sizes. Most of them were broken, having been excavated around the Majapahit capital of Wilwatikta (modern Trowulan). There are several examples on display at the National Museum of Indonesia in Jakarta, including this one to the right which was pieced back together from fragments by conservators.


In the 1920s, a new type of money box was launched by British banks, called the ‘home safe.’ The home safe was a little locked metal box given to customers, many of them children, to put their coins and banknotes in. The bank kept the key, so the customer had to take it to the bank to be unlocked. The money could put into bank account.





The structure of a money box is composed by a lid and a container.


When the box is full, by rotating the lid to open it, money can be all drawn out.


To judge the quantity of coins inside, shaking the box is a direct way to get instant feedback.

Affordance

The hole on the lid allows the user to put something into the box. The threadlike shape suggests that coins should be put in and only vertically. The hole is an affordance that conveys the possible function of this box, with the shape of the hole serving as con- straint of the coins.

Mapping

The mapping between the hole and coins is suggested by the hole. The threadlike shape provides logical con- straints – to fit the hole with a coin, you have to put it in a specific way. If not, the coin cannot get in, and the user will know that their operation is wrong.

Visability

The lid is distinct from the body of moneybox by comparison of colours and shapes, providing strong visual clues to the user that the lid can be opened by separating the two parts. The visibility indicates the mapping be- tween intended actions and operations.

Feedback

Coins collected in the moneybox will change the circumstances inside, thus cause different sounds when a new coin is put in. The sound gives the user instant feedback about whether the box still works.
